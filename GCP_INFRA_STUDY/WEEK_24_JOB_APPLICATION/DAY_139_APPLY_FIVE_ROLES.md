## Part 1 — Concept: Job Search Strategy (30 min)

### Job Search Channels

| Channel | Hit Rate | Best For | Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Internal Referrals** | 30-50% | Best conversion rate by far | Ask contacts at target companies, offer to share your portfolio |
| **LinkedIn Jobs** | 10-15% | Broad reach, recruiter visibility | Apply + InMail the recruiter or hiring manager |
| **Company Careers Pages** | 10-20% | Targeted, serious applications | Apply directly — fewer applicants than job boards |
| **Google Careers** | 5-10% | Google-specific opportunities | Long process but high-quality roles |
| **Indeed / CWJobs** | 5-10% | Volume, UK-specific roles | Filter carefully — many recruiter re-posts |
| **Recruiter Outreach** | 15-25% | When they come to you | Keep LinkedIn optimised, respond promptly |

### Qualifying Criteria: Should You Apply?

Use the **70% rule**: Apply if you match 70%+ of the requirements.

| JD Section | Your Match | Apply? |
|---|---|---|
| ≥70% of "Required" skills | ✓ Match most technical requirements | Yes |
| 50-69% match | ✓ Match core skills, some gaps | Yes, if gaps are learnable |
| <50% match | ✗ Major skill gaps | No — save your energy for better fits |
| All "Nice to have" are your gaps | Still OK if "Required" matches | Yes — "nice to have" means optional |

### Application Timing

| Factor | Optimal | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Day of week** | Tuesday-Thursday | Monday = inbox overflow, Friday = wind-down |
| **Time of day** | 9-11 AM local time | Your application is at the top of the ATS queue |
| **Job posting age** | Within first 7 days | After 2 weeks, they may have enough applicants |
| **Application velocity** | 5 quality apps/week | Better than 20 spray-and-pray |

### Application Personalisation Checklist

For each application, complete this in ~30 minutes:

| Step | Time | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Read JD thoroughly | 5 min | Highlight required skills, keywords, company details |
| 2. Copy base resume | 1 min | Create a new version |
| 3. Tailor summary | 5 min | Use JD language in your summary |
| 4. Reorder projects | 3 min | Most relevant project first |
| 5. Adjust 2-3 bullets | 5 min | Match keywords from JD |
| 6. Personalise cover letter | 10 min | "Why this company" paragraph |
| 7. Name files correctly | 1 min | `YourName_CompanyName.pdf` |

### Dealing with Recruiter Agencies

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Confirm the actual company before sharing your CV | Send your CV without knowing the end client |
| Ask about the salary range upfront | Accept "competitive" without a number |
| Check if the role is real (ask when it was posted) | Apply to re-posts of expired roles |
| Get the recruiter's direct contact details | Rely only on email submissions |
| Ask about the interview process timeline | Accept vague "we'll be in touch" |

## Part 2 — Hands-On Activity: Find & Apply to 5 Roles (60 min)

### Exercise 1 — Job Search (20 min)

Search for roles across multiple channels. Aim to find 8-10 potential roles, then select the best 5.

**Search terms to use:**
- "Cloud Infrastructure Engineer"
- "GCP Engineer"
- "Cloud Engineer Terraform"
- "Infrastructure Engineer Linux GCP"
- "Platform Engineer cloud"

**Platforms to check:**
1. LinkedIn Jobs (filter: UK, past week, experience level)
2. Google Careers (cloud + infrastructure)
3. Indeed/CWJobs (filter: location, salary range)
4. Company careers pages of companies you're interested in

For each potential role:
1. Read the full JD
2. Score your match percentage
3. Check posting date (prioritise recent)
4. Note the company and role in your tracker

### Exercise 2 — Select Top 5 & Qualify (10 min)

From your search results, select the 5 best roles based on:

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Skill match (≥70% required skills) | 30% |
| Company interest (culture, tech stack, growth) | 25% |
| Role quality (scope, level, team size) | 20% |
| Posting freshness (within 7 days) | 15% |
| Salary range alignment | 10% |

### Exercise 3 — Tailor & Submit (30 min)

For each of the 5 selected roles (~6 minutes each):

1. **Tailor resume** — adjust summary, reorder projects, tweak 2-3 bullets
2. **Personalise cover letter** — "why this company" paragraph
3. **Submit** via the appropriate channel
4. **Log in tracker** — date, company, role, version sent, status

**If applying via LinkedIn:** After submitting, find the hiring manager or recruiter on LinkedIn and send a short connection request or InMail:

> "Hi [Name], I've just applied for the [Role Title] position at [Company]. With 6 years of Linux infrastructure experience and GCP ACE certification, I believe I'd be a strong fit. I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss. Happy to share my GitHub portfolio: [link]"

## Part 3 — Revision: Key Takeaways (15 min)

- **70% rule:** Apply if you match 70%+ of required skills — don't self-screen too aggressively
- **Referrals have the highest conversion rate** — always check if you know someone at the company
- **5 quality applications per week** beats 20 generic submissions
- **Apply Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM** for optimal ATS queue positioning
- **Apply within first 7 days** of job posting for best results
- **30 minutes per application:** read JD, tailor resume, personalise cover letter, submit
- **Track every application** in a spreadsheet — date, company, version, status, follow-up date
- **Follow up after 2 weeks** with a short, professional message
- **Search for multiple role titles** — Cloud Engineer, Platform Engineer, DevOps, SRE are all adjacent
- **Quality over quantity** — one well-researched, tailored application >> ten generic ones

## Part 4 — Quiz (15 min)

**Q1.** A job posting lists 10 required skills. You match 7 of them confidently, 2 partially, and 1 not at all. Should you apply?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Yes, absolutely.** You match 7/10 (70%) confidently and 2 more partially — that's effectively 80-90% match. The **70% rule** says apply. Missing 1 skill out of 10 is very common and rarely disqualifying alone.

Key considerations:
1. **Which skill is missing?** If it's "Kubernetes" but everything else matches, many companies will consider you — they can teach Kubernetes to someone with strong infrastructure fundamentals.
2. **Partially matched skills still count.** If you have "some CI/CD experience" and the JD says "CI/CD pipelines", your partial experience + willingness to deepen is enough.
3. **JDs are wish lists**, not hard minimums. Hiring managers write aspirational descriptions. They'd rather hire a strong 70% match who can grow than wait 6 months for a perfect 100% match who doesn't exist.

Only skip if the missing skill is the **core of the role** (e.g., "5 years Kubernetes experience required" for a Kubernetes-focused role and you have zero exposure).
</details>

**Q2.** You've been applying for 2 weeks with no callbacks. What should you evaluate?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Systematic troubleshooting:

1. **ATS issues:** Is your resume ATS-friendly? Test by pasting into a plain text file. If formatting breaks, fix it. Remove columns, graphics, and fancy formatting.

2. **Keyword match:** Compare your resume keywords to the JD keywords. Are you using the exact phrases from JDs? "Infrastructure as Code" not "I use Terraform."

3. **Resume quality:** Have someone else review your resume. Common issues: no metrics, generic bullets, unclear what you actually did vs what the team did.

4. **Application quality:** Are you tailoring each application, or sending the same generic resume? Tailored applications convert 3-5x better.

5. **Role targeting:** Are you applying to the right level? If you're only targeting Senior Cloud Architect roles, you might need to target mid-level Cloud Engineer roles instead.

6. **Volume:** 2 weeks is early. Most companies take 2-4 weeks to review applications. Keep applying while waiting for responses. 5 well-tailored applications per week means 10 after 2 weeks — that's a reasonable sample. After 20-25 applications with zero callbacks, it's time to make changes.
</details>

**Q3.** What's the advantage of applying directly on a company's careers page vs through a job board?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

1. **Fewer applicants** — job boards aggregate and amplify, meaning 500+ people might apply via LinkedIn. The company's own careers page typically has fewer applicants because it requires more effort to find and navigate.

2. **Direct into their ATS** — applying through job boards sometimes creates formatting issues or data loss. Direct applications go straight into the company's ATS without a middleman.

3. **Signal of genuine interest** — finding and applying through the careers page shows you researched the company, not just clicked "Easy Apply" on LinkedIn.

4. **Sometimes different roles** — companies don't always post all positions on LinkedIn or Indeed. Their careers page may have roles not listed elsewhere.

**Best practice:** Apply via the company careers page AND connect with the recruiter/hiring manager on LinkedIn. This gives you two touchpoints: the formal application and a personal connection.
</details>

**Q4.** After submitting an application, what should you do in the following 2 weeks?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Week 1 (Days 1-7):**
1. **Connect on LinkedIn** with the recruiter or hiring manager (if not done already)
2. **Continue applying** to other roles — never wait for one application
3. **Research the company** — prepare for a potential phone screen: their tech stack, recent news, interview process (check Glassdoor)
4. **Continue studying** — your prep shouldn't stop while waiting

**Week 2 (Days 8-14):**
1. **Follow up** at the 2-week mark with a short, professional message (email to recruiter or LinkedIn message)
2. **Update your tracker** with any status changes
3. **Analyse patterns** — if you've applied to 10+ roles with no callbacks, review your materials (see Q2)
4. **Keep applying** — maintain 5 quality applications per week

**General rules:**
- Never follow up more than once (unless they respond and conversation continues)
- Don't call unless specifically asked to
- Stay positive and professional in all communications
- Log everything in your tracker for pattern analysis
</details>
