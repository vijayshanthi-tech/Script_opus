## Part 1 — Concept: LinkedIn for Cloud Engineers (30 min)

### LinkedIn vs Resume: Key Differences

| Element | Resume | LinkedIn |
|---|---|---|
| **Length** | 1-2 pages max | No strict limit — more detail is fine |
| **Tone** | Formal, concise | Professional but personable |
| **Summary** | 3-4 lines | Full paragraph + career narrative |
| **Projects** | 2 bullet points each | Can include images, links, descriptions |
| **Updates** | Tailored per application | One version, optimised for search |
| **Keywords** | Targeted per JD | Broad — cover all target JD terms |
| **Audience** | Hiring managers | Recruiters searching by keyword |

### Headline Formula

Your headline appears in search results, comments, and messages. It's the most visible text.

**Formula:** `[Target Role] | [Key Differentiator] | [Credential]`

**Examples:**

| Headline | Strength |
|---|---|
| `Cloud Infrastructure Engineer | ACE Certified | 6 Years Linux + GCP` | Clear role, cert, experience |
| `GCP Cloud Engineer | Terraform & Linux Infrastructure | ACE Certified` | Technology-focused |
| `Infrastructure Engineer → Cloud | ACE Certified | Terraform, GCP, SRE` | Shows transition intentionally |

**Don't use:** "Aspiring Cloud Engineer" (signals unready), "Open to Work" alone (needs context), your current job title if it doesn't match your target.

### About Section Structure

The About section is your **extended pitch**. Structure it in 4 paragraphs:

**Paragraph 1 — Who You Are (3 sentences)**
Your identity, background, and what you do now.

**Paragraph 2 — What You Bring (3-4 sentences)**
Key skills, technologies, and the value you offer.

**Paragraph 3 — What You've Built (3-4 sentences)**
Portfolio highlights and evidence of hands-on work.

**Paragraph 4 — What's Next (2 sentences)**
Where you're heading and what you're looking for.

**Template:**

```
Cloud Infrastructure Engineer with 6 years of hands-on Linux systems administration 
and a proven track record of managing production infrastructure at scale. Transitioned 
into GCP cloud engineering with an ACE certification and a portfolio of production-grade 
infrastructure projects.

My core strengths span infrastructure as code (Terraform), GCP networking (VPC, Cloud NAT, 
firewall architecture), identity and access management (drawing on 3 years of RHDS/LDAP 
experience), and observability (Cloud Monitoring, SLO/SLI, alerting). I bring deep OS-level 
expertise — hardening, patching, capacity planning — that strengthens my cloud infrastructure work.

I've built and documented a portfolio of GCP projects demonstrating progression from secure VM 
provisioning to VPC architecture to full SRE monitoring packs. Each project is implemented in 
Terraform, thoroughly documented, and published on GitHub with architecture diagrams and 
troubleshooting guides.

Currently targeting Cloud Infrastructure / Platform Engineer roles where I can combine my 
Linux foundation with GCP cloud engineering skills. Open to conversations about infrastructure, 
SRE, and DevOps opportunities.

🔗 GitHub: github.com/yourusername
📧 your.email@example.com
```

### Experience Section: Different from Resume

| Resume Bullets | LinkedIn Bullets |
|---|---|
| Concise, metric-heavy | Can be more narrative |
| 4-6 bullets per role | 3-5 bullets, slightly longer |
| Tailored per application | One version, comprehensive |
| More formal | Can include context and learning |

**LinkedIn Experience Example:**

```
Systems Administrator | Company Name | 2020 - Present

Managing 200+ RHEL production servers across multiple datacentres, 
with focus on reliability, security, and automation.

• Manage fleet of 200+ RHEL 7/8 servers — patching, hardening, and 
  capacity planning maintaining 99.9% uptime
• Designed and operated RHDS/LDAP directory serving 5,000+ users with 
  automated provisioning and access control

  from 4 hours to 30 minutes

• Mentored 3 junior admins on Linux troubleshooting and best practices
```

### Featured Section

LinkedIn's Featured section lets you pin content at the top of your profile. Use it for:

| Featured Item | How to Add | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **GitHub portfolio link** | Add as link | Direct proof of work |
| **Best project README** | Share as post or link | Shows documentation skills |
| **ACE certification** | Add as image or link | Credibility |
| **Technical blog post** (if you write one) | Share as article | Thought leadership |

### Skills & Endorsements Strategy

Add and order skills strategically:

| Priority | Skills to Add |
|---|---|
| **Top 3 (pin these)** | Google Cloud Platform, Terraform, Linux |
| **Core cloud** | Cloud Infrastructure, Cloud Networking, IAM, Cloud Monitoring |
| **IaC/automation** | Infrastructure as Code, Bash Scripting, Python, CI/CD |
| **Operations** | SRE, Incident Response, Systems Administration |
| **Soft skills** | Technical Documentation, Mentoring, Problem Solving |

**Endorsement strategy:** Ask 3-5 colleagues to endorse your top skills. Reciprocate for theirs.

### Making Your Profile Recruiter-Friendly

| Setting | What to Do |
|---|---|
| **Open to Work** | Enable privately (visible to recruiters only) — select "Cloud Engineer", region, etc. |
| **Profile photo** | Professional headshot, clear face, neutral background |
| **Background banner** | GCP/cloud themed or professional (not default grey) |
| **Custom URL** | Set to: linkedin.com/in/yourname (no random numbers) |
| **Location** | Set to your target job market |
| **Industry** | "Information Technology & Services" or "Computer & Network Security" |

## Part 4 — Quiz (15 min)

**Q1.** Why is "Aspiring Cloud Engineer" a bad LinkedIn headline, and what should you use instead?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

"Aspiring" signals to recruiters that you're **not yet qualified** — it positions you as a student, not a professional. Recruiters search for people who **are** cloud engineers, not people who want to be one. 

**Better alternatives:**
- `Cloud Infrastructure Engineer | ACE Certified | 6 Years Linux + GCP` — positions you as the role
- `Infrastructure Engineer — Linux to Cloud | ACE Certified | Terraform & GCP` — honest about transition but confident

You have an ACE certification, 6 years of infrastructure experience, and a portfolio of projects. You **are** a Cloud Infrastructure Engineer — you're just looking for a company to do it for. Drop "aspiring" and own the title.
</details>

**Q2.** How should your LinkedIn About section differ from your resume summary?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Key differences:
1. **Length:** Resume summary = 3-5 lines. LinkedIn About = 4 paragraphs (~150-200 words)
2. **Tone:** Resume = formal and dense. LinkedIn = professional but personable, can use "I"
3. **Content:** Resume tailored per JD. LinkedIn = comprehensive, optimised for broad search
4. **Narrative:** Resume = bullet achievements. LinkedIn = tell a story (background → skills → evidence → goals)
5. **Contact info:** Resume has this in the header. LinkedIn About should also include GitHub link and email
6. **Call to action:** Resume doesn't have one. LinkedIn should: "Open to conversations about infrastructure/SRE opportunities"

LinkedIn is the place to show **personality and narrative** that a resume can't convey. It's where you explain your "why" — why you're transitioning, why you're passionate about cloud infrastructure, why you'd be a great addition to a team.
</details>

**Q3.** You have 25 skills listed on LinkedIn. How should you prioritise and manage them?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Pin your top 3:** The skills you want to be found for — GCP, Terraform, Linux. These appear prominently.

**Order the rest by relevance:**
- Tier 1 (skills 4-10): Other job-relevant skills: Cloud Infrastructure, IAM, Networking, Bash, Monitoring
- Tier 2 (skills 11-20): Supporting skills: Python, CI/CD, Documentation, SRE, Docker
- Tier 3 (skills 21-25): Foundational skills: Git, Problem Solving, Agile

**Remove irrelevant skills:** If you have "Microsoft Word" or "COBOL" listed, remove them — they'll pollute your search visibility and signal the wrong expertise.

**Get endorsements for top skills:** Ask 3-5 colleagues to endorse GCP, Terraform, and Linux specifically. Skills with endorsements rank higher in recruiter searches.
</details>

**Q4.** A recruiter searches LinkedIn for "Cloud Infrastructure Engineer Terraform GCP London." Which profile elements determine whether you appear in results?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

LinkedIn search indexes these elements (in approximate priority order):

1. **Headline** — "Cloud Infrastructure Engineer" in your headline = strongest match
2. **Skills** — "Terraform" and "GCP" listed and pinned
3. **Location** — "London" or nearby with "Open to Work" settings matching London
4. **Current job title** — in Experience section
5. **About section** — keywords throughout the text
6. **Experience bullets** — mentions of technologies and skills

To appear for this search: (1) Have "Cloud Infrastructure Engineer" in headline, (2) "Terraform" and "GCP" as pinned skills with endorsements, (3) Location set to London/UK, (4) These terms also in your About section and Experience bullets, (5) "Open to Work" enabled for "Cloud Engineer" roles in the London area. Missing any of these reduces your ranking in search results.
</details>
